Executive Summary

This dashboard explores how coverage gaps may disrupt continuity of care and worsen patient outcomes. In this analysis, 39.5% of patients experienced a coverage gap, with an average gap length of 23.1 days. 
Patients with gaps showed higher average severity, and severity increased as gap duration lengthened. Post-gap ER utilization reached 24.6%, suggesting that delayed or interrupted access to care may contribute 
to avoidable downstream utilization.  ￼

These findings support a simple operational idea: insurance renewal should be treated as a care continuity issue, not just an administrative task. Proactive reminders, outreach workflows, and targeted follow-up 
for high-risk patients could reduce lapse-related disruptions and improve outcomes. This creates a strong use case for healthcare organizations to monitor coverage gaps as a decision-support metric.  ￼
